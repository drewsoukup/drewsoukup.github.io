Nikola Jokic Impacts Basketball More Than Anyone of Our Lifetime

We’ve seen players who are on another level in the NBA, but none other impact the game offensively in every facet the way Nikola Jokic does. Night after night he’s stuffing stat sheets averaging 21.8 points, 10.9 rebounds and 7.2 assists across his decade-long career. A DECADE of almost a triple-double every night on average. There’s a reason why he’s won 3 NBA MVP awards.

But it’s not just individual accolades Jokic has achieved, he’s lead the charge of historically good team basketball for the Denver Nuggets. Jokic has lead them to six consecutive playoff appearances winning a championship as well in 2023. His unselfishness and willingness to make the right play to win has lead the Nuggets offense to be in a stratosphere of their own. Even when comparing to the last 5 other champions in the NBA, over those 5 years the Nuggets blow them out of the water offensively.

In our lifetime (2003 – Present) we haven’t seen someone who impacts an offense quite like Jokic over his prime. Comparing 2020-2025 Jokic to some of the greats of the 21st century shows how dominant and impactful he is. Even amongst a list of 2014-2018 Steph Curry, 2003-2007 Kobe Bryant, 2009-2013 Lebron James, 2018-2022 Giannis Antetokounmpo, 2019-2023 Joel Embiid, 2016-2020 James Harden and 2013-2017 Kevin Durant, Nikola Jokic stands out as one of the most impactful half-decades offensively we’ve seen. Jokic’s impact for the Nuggets when on the floor vs off is wider than any of these primes we’ve seen to this day.

Mr. Triple-Double himself can’t help but put himself on all-time lists year after year. In season PRA (Points + Rebounds + Assists) since 2003, he’s landed in the top 10 three times. The only other players to make it twice are James Harden and Kobe Bryant who both averaged over 30 points in their respective seasons. Jokic impact is not just a one time fluke, it’s year after year after year.

library(tidyverse)
library(hoopR)
library(gt)

stats <- load_nba_player_box(seasons = 2003:2025) |> 
    filter(team_display_name != "Team Giannis") |> 
  filter(team_display_name != "Team LeBron") |>
  filter(team_display_name != "Western Conf All-Stars") |>
  filter(team_display_name != "Eastern Conf All-Stars") |>
  filter(team_display_name != "USA") |>
  filter(team_display_name != "World") |> 
   filter(team_display_name != "Team Shaq") |> 
  filter(team_display_name != "Team Kenny")

player_pras <- stats |>
  group_by(season, athlete_display_name, team_display_name) |>
  summarise(
    pra = sum(points, assists, rebounds),
    pts_avg = mean(points)
  ) |> 
  filter(pra > 4000) |> 
  arrange(desc(pra))

current_teamlogs <- load_nba_team_box(seasons = 2003:2025) |> 
  filter(
  (season == "2018" & team_display_name == "Cleveland Cavaliers") |
  (season == "2024" & team_display_name == "Dallas Mavericks") |
  (season == "2019" & team_display_name == "Houston Rockets") |
  (season == "2017" & team_display_name == "Houston Rockets") |
  (season == "2024" & team_display_name == "Denver Nuggets") |
  (season == "2003" & team_display_name == "San Antonio Spurs") |
  (season == "2004" & team_display_name == "Minnesota Timberwolves") |
  (season == "2023" & team_display_name == "Denver Nuggets") |
  (season == "2008" & team_display_name == "Los Angeles Lakers") |
  (season == "2025" & team_display_name == "Denver Nuggets") |
  (season == "2023" & team_display_name == "Boston Celtics") |
  (season == "2003" & team_display_name == "Los Angeles Lakers"))

team_pras <- current_teamlogs |>
  group_by(season, team_display_name) |>
  summarise(
    team_pra = sum(team_score, assists, total_rebounds)
  ) |> 
  arrange(desc(team_pra))

pra_pct <- player_pras |>
  left_join(team_pras, by = c("season", "team_display_name")) |>
  mutate(
    pra_share = pra / team_pra,
    pra_pct = pra_share * 100 
  ) |> 
  select(athlete_display_name, pra_pct, pts_avg) |> 
  arrange(desc(pra_pct))

pra_pct |> 
  ungroup() |>
  gt() |> 
  cols_label(
